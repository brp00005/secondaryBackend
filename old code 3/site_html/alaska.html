Statewide Chamber Organization

Alaska Chamber

About: The Alaska Chamber is the statewide business organization dedicated to improving the business environment throughout Alaska. Representing hundreds of businesses from Ketchikan to Barrow, the Chamber shares a common goal to make Alaska a viable and competitive place to do business. Originally formed in 1952 as the "All Alaska" Chamber of Commerce prior to statehood, the name was changed to the Alaska State Chamber of Commerce with statehood.

Address: 2550 Denali Street, Suite 530, Anchorage, AK 99503

Phone: (907) 278-2722

Email: [email protected]

Website: alaskachamber.com

Leadership: Kati Capozzi, President and CEO

Governance: Board of Directors with 80 members including 20 elected representatives from each Alaska Senate District, 10 elected At-Large representatives, 14 appointed members, and representatives from Alaska's 40+ local chambers

Services: Statewide business advocacy, legislative representation in Juneau and Washington D.C., grassroots and committee participation, networking opportunities, Capitol Notes updates, member discounts

Resources for Alaska Businesses

Unique Alaska Business Challenges

Alaska chambers address unique challenges faced by businesses operating in America's largest state:

  • Remote Locations: Supporting businesses in communities accessible only by water or air
  • Transportation Costs: Advocating for infrastructure improvements including roads, rail, aviation, and water access
  • Seasonal Business: Supporting tourism-dependent businesses through shoulder seasons
  • Workforce Development: Addressing labor shortages in remote regions
  • Cost of Living: Working to maintain competitive business environment despite high costs
  • Military Relations: Promoting cooperation between military and business communities

Key Economic Sectors

Alaska chambers support diverse industries across the state:

  • Tourism & Hospitality: Supporting Alaska's $4.5 billion tourism industry
  • Oil & Gas: Advocating for energy sector that drives state economy
  • Fishing & Seafood: Supporting world-class commercial fishing industry
  • Military & Defense: Serving communities near Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, Fort Wainwright, Eielson AFB
  • Mining: Supporting responsible resource development
  • Transportation & Logistics: Advocacy for Alaska as strategic transportation hub
  • Healthcare: Addressing rural healthcare access and costs

Regional Economic Development

  • Business Recruitment: Attracting new companies to Alaska communities
  • Retention Programs: Supporting existing business growth and expansion
  • Infrastructure Advocacy: Transportation, broadband, utilities enhancement
  • Workforce Solutions: Connecting employers with Alaska talent
  • Tourism Promotion: Marketing Alaska destinations and experiences
  • Quality of Life: Enhancing communities to attract and retain workers

Frequently Asked Questions

The Alaska Chamber is the statewide business organization that represents hundreds of businesses from across Alaska and advocates for pro-business policies at the state and federal levels in Juneau and Washington D.C. Local chambers of commerce serve specific communities or regions (like Anchorage, Fairbanks, or Juneau) and focus on local business networking, community development, and regional economic issues. Many businesses hold memberships in both their local chamber and the Alaska Chamber to benefit from both local connections and statewide advocacy. The Alaska Chamber's Board includes representatives from Alaska's 40+ local chambers.

Chamber membership costs in Alaska vary by chamber size and business type. Small business memberships typically range from $250 to $600 annually for local chambers, while larger chambers like Anchorage, Fairbanks, and Juneau may charge $500 to $2,500+ for small businesses depending on employee count. Corporate memberships for larger companies can range from $2,000 to $10,000+ annually. The statewide Alaska Chamber offers tiered membership based on company size. Most chambers provide different membership levels with varying benefits. Contact your local chamber directly for specific pricing information, as costs reflect the unique challenges of operating in Alaska's remote communities and high-cost environment.

Primary benefits include networking opportunities through Business After Hours events and weekly forums, access to elected officials at state and federal levels, aggressive advocacy in Juneau and Washington D.C. on Alaska business issues, instant business credibility in your community, member directory listings and marketing opportunities, free seminars and educational workshops, demographic data and market research, member-exclusive discounts and programs, and connections to Alaska's business network of 1,800+ member companies. The Anchorage Chamber offers 40+ years of established Business After Hours networking, while chambers throughout Alaska provide critical support for businesses facing unique Alaska challenges like remote locations, high transportation costs, and seasonal operations.

If your business operates across multiple Alaska regions, consider joining your primary local chamber plus the statewide Alaska Chamber. For example, a business based in Anchorage but serving statewide might join the Anchorage Chamber for local networking and the Alaska Chamber for statewide advocacy. Businesses operating in both Anchorage and Fairbanks might join both regional chambers plus the state chamber. Tourism businesses serving multiple communities often join chambers along their service routes. The Alaska Chamber provides statewide advocacy regardless of location, while local chambers offer community-specific networking and market access. Evaluate each chamber's geographic coverage, networking events, and advocacy priorities to determine the best combination for your business needs.

You can find your local Alaska chamber through several resources: Visit the Alaska Chamber website at alaskachamber.com and check their member directory which includes representatives from 40+ local chambers; use the U.S. Chamber of Commerce Alaska finder at uschamber.com/co/chambers/alaska which lists chambers by city; search online for "[City Name] Alaska chamber of commerce"; or contact the Alaska Chamber directly at (907) 278-2722 for referrals to local chambers. Note that some remote Alaska communities may not have formal chambers but may have business associations or economic development organizations. Visitor centers in tourism communities often house chamber offices and can provide business information.

Alaska chambers offer diverse networking opportunities adapted to Alaska's unique environment. The Anchorage Chamber hosts Business After Hours monthly evening mixers rotating between member businesses (running 40+ years), Weekly Forums every Monday at noon at the Dena'ina and Egan Centers with networking starting at 11:30 am, and speed networking events throughout the year. Fairbanks Chamber organizes industry-specific networking groups and community events. Seasonal variations are common, with more frequent events during summer tourism season and winter networking focused on year-round residents. Many chambers host annual events like awards galas, golf tournaments (when weather permits), and holiday gatherings. Remote communities may offer quarterly networking events coordinated with legislative sessions or when access is easier.

Yes, Alaska chambers provide extensive support for seasonal businesses and tourism operators, which are vital to Alaska's economy. Chambers in tourism destinations like Juneau, Ketchikan, Skagway, Seward, Denali, and Talkeetna offer tourism-specific programs including visitor center operations, cruise ship passenger welcome programs, marketing of local attractions, seasonal workforce recruitment assistance, and advocacy for extended tourism seasons. Many chambers offer discounted winter memberships for summer-only businesses. The Alaska Chamber advocates at the state level for policies supporting the $4.5 billion tourism industry. Chambers also help tourism businesses diversify into shoulder season activities and connect with year-round revenue opportunities. Visitor center operations by chambers in Soldotna, Seward, and other communities directly support tourism member businesses.

Alaska chambers actively address challenges unique to operating businesses in remote locations. They advocate for improved transportation infrastructure including aviation, maritime access, and road connections critical for communities accessible only by air or water. Chambers work on broadband and telecommunications expansion to enable modern business operations in remote areas. They address high shipping and supply costs through infrastructure advocacy and bulk purchasing programs. Workforce development initiatives help remote businesses recruit and retain employees despite isolation challenges. Chambers coordinate with military installations on economic development opportunities. They provide virtual networking options for businesses unable to travel frequently to regional hubs. The Greater Fairbanks Chamber's Transportation and Infrastructure Committee specifically advocates for Northern Region transportation improvements to enhance Fairbanks as Alaska's transportation hub.

The Alaska Native Chamber is a specialized organization focused on Alaska Native businesses, entrepreneurs, and industry leaders. It provides access to a dynamic network specifically supporting Native-owned businesses and economic development in Alaska Native communities. Membership benefits include business-to-business connections to form strategic partnerships, regional, national, and international exposure through business forums and events, exclusive member events designed to drive collaboration and new ventures, and connections to Native corporation resources and opportunities. The chamber is ideal for Alaska Native-owned businesses, entrepreneurs seeking to do business with Native corporations, companies interested in Alaska Native economic development, and organizations supporting Alaska Native communities. The chamber can be reached at (907) 201-2626. Both Native and non-Native businesses interested in supporting Alaska Native economic development are welcome.

The U.S. Chamber of Commerce 5-Star Accreditation is awarded to chambers demonstrating excellence in governance, finance, programs, and community impact. Only 187 chambers out of 7,500 nationwide (top 3%) hold this elite accreditation. The Anchorage Chamber of Commerce has earned 5-Star Accreditation, demonstrating its commitment to professional standards and effective operations. Accreditation benefits members by ensuring the chamber follows sound policies, implements effective procedures, maintains strong financial management, delivers quality programs and services, and creates positive community impact. For businesses, joining an accredited chamber provides confidence in the organization's professionalism, access to best practices from top-performing chambers nationwide, and assurance that membership dues are managed responsibly. While accreditation is not required, it indicates a chamber's dedication to excellence and member value.
