Small Business Resources

Connecticut Small Business Development Center (CTSBDC)

Professional expert advisors offer guidance and support to help small business owners reach goals. Services include accessing funding, creating new strategies, and finding critical information.

Partnership: Funded through cooperative agreement with U.S. Small Business Administration, CT Department of Economic and Community Development, and UConn.

Website: ctsbdc.uconn.edu

Connecticut Small Business Boost Fund

Allows small businesses and nonprofits to borrow between $5,000 and $500,000 depending on eligibility and need.

Supported by: Department of Economic and Community Development (DECD)

Additional Resources: Office of Small Business Affairs connects business owners with resources for growth and relocation support.

Norwalk Business Development Center

All-inclusive free resource center for entrepreneurs and business owners throughout business lifecycle.

Location: 3 Belden Avenue, Norwalk, CT

Partners: City of Norwalk, Greater Norwalk Hispanic Chamber, Greater Norwalk Chamber, SCORE, Small Business Development Council, Norwalk Redevelopment Agency, Women's Business Development Council, and NAACP

Services: All resources available free of charge to ensure accessibility for everyone.

SBA Connecticut District Office

Federal support for Connecticut's 360,000+ small businesses including funding programs, mentorship, and business recovery centers.

Annual Recognition: Connecticut Small Business Person of the Year awarded by SBA and CBIA, honoring exceptional small business leaders for achievements and contributions.

Resources: Small Business Profiles provide annual portraits of state's smaller employers including economic activity snapshots.

Frequently Asked Questions

Connecticut has 70 chambers of commerce serving businesses across all eight counties. These range from the statewide Connecticut Business & Industry Association (CBIA) to regional chambers like the Chamber of Commerce of Eastern CT with 1,400+ members, to local city and town chambers. Major metropolitan areas like Hartford, New Haven, Bridgeport, Stamford, and Norwalk each have their own dedicated chambers serving hundreds to thousands of member businesses.

The Connecticut Business & Industry Association (CBIA) serves as the state's primary business organization and chamber, located at 350 Church St, Hartford, CT 06103. Contact them at (860) 244-1900 or visit www.cbia.com. CBIA is Connecticut's largest business organization and a U.S. Chamber of Commerce member, providing statewide advocacy, legislative representation, policy research, and business resources for the state's 360,000+ small businesses.

The largest chambers include: (1) Central Connecticut Chambers of Commerce with 1,600+ members serving 160,000 residents across seven communities; (2) Chamber of Commerce of Eastern CT with 1,400+ active members spanning eastern Connecticut; (3) Bridgeport Regional Business Council with 800 members and six affiliates; (4) Greater Danbury Chamber with 500+ members representing a workforce of 105,000 people. The MetroHartford Alliance and Greater New Haven Chamber also rank among the state's most influential business organizations.

Connecticut chambers offer extensive benefits including networking opportunities through Business After Hours events, Coffee Connect mornings, and 100+ annual events; marketing support via member directories, website listings, email spotlights, and social media promotion; business resources like sales leads, professional referrals, training programs, and exclusive discounts; advocacy through legislative representation at state and local levels; ribbon cutting ceremonies with media coverage; access to business resource centers; and professional development programs. Some chambers host 125+ networking events annually with opportunities morning, midday, and after hours.

Connecticut offers robust small business support including the Connecticut Small Business Development Center (CTSBDC) providing free expert guidance for funding, strategy, and growth; Connecticut Small Business Boost Fund offering loans of $5,000-$500,000; Norwalk Business Development Center providing all-inclusive free resources through partnerships with the city, chambers, SCORE, and NAACP; SBA Connecticut District Office offering federal funding, mentorship, and recovery centers; Office of Small Business Affairs connecting owners with growth resources; and chamber-operated business resource centers with startup kits, business plan templates, tax information, and specialized materials for various industries. Greater New Haven Chamber's resource page is available to all businesses, even non-members.

The MetroHartford Alliance serves the Hartford region spanning Hartford, Middlesex, and Tolland counties. Located at 31 Pratt St, Hartford, CT 06103 (recently relocated to the city's tallest office tower), this top-performing economic development partnership executes innovative strategies for growing the regional economy. The organization recently launched websites for the reactivated Hartford Chamber of Commerce, Connecticut Insurance & Financial Services (CT IFS), Connecticut Health Council, and Innovation Destination Hartford. Visit www.metrohartford.com for information about membership, which includes discounted rates to events hosted by both the Hartford Chamber and MetroHartford Alliance.

Connecticut's 70 chambers are organized by region and county. Major cities have dedicated chambers: Hartford (MetroHartford Alliance), New Haven (Greater New Haven Chamber), Bridgeport (Bridgeport Regional Business Council), Stamford (Stamford Chamber), Norwalk (Greater Norwalk Chamber), Danbury (Greater Danbury Chamber), and Waterbury (Waterbury Regional Chamber). Regional chambers serve multi-town areas like Central Connecticut Chambers (Bristol, Burlington, Farmington, Plainville, Plymouth, Thomaston, Wolcott) and Chamber of Commerce of Eastern CT (Waterford and eastern region). Use the U.S. Chamber's Connecticut Chamber Finder at uschamber.com/co/chambers/connecticut or contact CBIA for referrals to local chambers serving your area.

The Greater New Haven Chamber of Commerce, founded in 1794, is one of the oldest chambers of commerce in the United States. For over two centuries, this chamber has been influential in shaping public policy and fostering business growth in the New Haven region. Today it serves 15 municipalities including New Haven, Branford, Cheshire, East Haven, Guilford, Hamden, Madison, Milford, North Branford, North Haven, Orange, Wallingford, West Haven, and Woodbridge. The chamber provides advocacy, networking, and support to startups, small businesses, and established enterprises. Contact them at 900 Chapel St, 10th Floor, New Haven, CT 06510, (203) 787-6735, or visit www.gnhcc.com.

Connecticut chambers host extensive networking opportunities. Business After Hours (BAH) events are scheduled monthly from 5:30-7:30 PM with 40-50 attendees. Coffee Connect events meet the first Tuesday of each month from 8:00-9:00 AM with similar attendance. West Hartford Chamber offers 125+ networking and business development events annually with morning, midday, and after hours options. Events include educational programs, committee meetings, leadership councils, industry coalitions (like Manufacturers' Coalition), Women's Leadership Council gatherings, and ribbon cutting ceremonies. The Fairfield Chamber hosts monthly networking averaging 40-50 people per event. Most chambers provide both formal structured events and casual networking opportunities throughout the year.

Connecticut has more than 360,000 small businesses statewide according to the U.S. Small Business Administration's Small Business Profiles. These businesses span diverse sectors from manufacturing and hi-tech to retail, service, professional, and corporate enterprises, ranging from sole proprietors to companies with thousands of employees. The state provides extensive support through the Department of Economic and Community Development, Connecticut Small Business Development Center, SBA Connecticut District Office, and the network of 70 chambers of commerce. CBIA and SBA annually honor the Connecticut Small Business Person of the Year, recognizing exceptional leaders for their achievements and contributions to the state's economy.
