Statewide Leadership: The Business & Industry Association of New Hampshire serves as the state's chamber of commerce, representing over 400 members and 89,000 employees statewide. Working collaboratively with 45 local and regional chambers coordinated through NHACCE (New Hampshire Association of Chamber of Commerce Executives), the network provides comprehensive business advocacy, networking, and economic development services across all 10 counties.

State Chamber of Commerce

Business & Industry Association of New Hampshire (BIA)

Address: 122 North Main Street, Concord, NH 03301

Phone: (603) 224-5388

Website: biaofnh.com

President & CEO: Michael Skelton

Members: 400+ member companies across industries

Employment Impact: Members employ 89,000 people (1 in 7 jobs statewide)

New Hampshire's statewide chamber of commerce and leading business advocate. Through advocacy with state legislators and regulators, BIA shapes business-friendly public policy and provides counterbalance to legislation and regulations that threaten the growth and prosperity of New Hampshire businesses. The only broad-based business advocacy organization providing direct connection to the legislative process.

Industry Representation

Members represent diverse sectors including advanced manufacturing, high technology, professional services, financial services, health care, hospitality and tourism, public utilities, higher education, and insurance.

2024 Public Policy Priorities

  • Fiscal Responsibility: Supporting fiscally conservative state budgets with business-friendly regulatory and business tax policies
  • Tax Policy: Opposing income tax or sales tax; opposing increases to Business Enterprise Tax or Business Profits Tax rates
  • Housing: Supporting efforts to increase availability and affordability of housing for working people
  • Workforce Development: Supporting higher quality educational and training opportunities, Career and Technical Education, experiential learning, apprenticeships, and internships

Member Benefits

  • Legislative advocacy and representation at state level
  • Access to key business and political insights through BIA communications and publications
  • Networking with business leaders, policymakers, and opinion leaders
  • Energy buying program for cost savings
  • Discount prescription drug program
  • Member-to-member discount opportunities
  • Annual Dinner and Awards Celebration - NH's largest business networking event
  • Professional education and training seminars and conferences

Frequently Asked Questions

The Business & Industry Association of New Hampshire (BIA) serves as the state's chamber of commerce, representing over 400 member companies that employ 89,000 people (1 in 7 jobs statewide). BIA provides statewide business advocacy, legislative representation, and member services including energy buying programs, discount prescription drug programs, and professional education. Additionally, there are approximately 45 local and regional chambers across the state coordinated through NHACCE (New Hampshire Association of Chamber of Commerce Executives), each serving specific communities and regions.

New Hampshire has approximately 45 local and regional chambers of commerce serving communities across all 10 counties. Major chambers include Greater Manchester (nearly 1,000 members), Greater Concord (nearly 900 members), Greater Nashua, Chamber Collaborative of Greater Portsmouth, Lakes Region Chamber (525+ members), Hampton Area, Greater Rochester (450 members), Upper Valley Business Alliance (500+ businesses), and Greater Monadnock Collaborative. Each chamber operates independently while collaborating through NHACCE to provide comprehensive business support statewide.

Chamber members receive extensive networking opportunities through Business After Hours events, coffee connections, and annual dinners. Marketing benefits include online directory listings, print directory distribution, social media promotion, and newsletter features. Members gain advocacy representation at state and local government levels on business climate, taxation, and regulation issues. Educational benefits include business training seminars, economic outlook presentations, and leadership development programs. Additional perks include member-to-member discounts, ribbon cutting ceremonies, access to business intelligence, and connections to decision-makers and community leaders.

Visit the BIA website at biaofnh.com/chambers/ for a complete directory of New Hampshire chambers organized by region. You can also use the U.S. Chamber finder at uschamber.com/co/chambers/new-hampshire. For specific regions: Greater Manchester serves 10 communities in southern NH; Greater Concord covers the capital region; Lakes Region Chamber serves 30+ communities in central NH; Chamber Collaborative of Greater Portsmouth covers the Seacoast; Upper Valley Business Alliance serves the Lebanon-Hanover area; and Greater Monadnock Collaborative serves southwest NH. Contact the state BIA office at (603) 224-5388 for personalized referrals.

BIA provides statewide legislative advocacy representing member interests to state legislators and regulators, shaping business-friendly public policy. BIA's 2024 priorities include supporting fiscally conservative budgets, opposing income and sales taxes, preventing Business Enterprise Tax and Business Profits Tax increases, supporting housing affordability initiatives, and promoting workforce development through Career and Technical Education, apprenticeships, and internships. Local chambers advocate at municipal levels on zoning, economic development, and community issues. Chambers host public policy roundtables gathering business leader input, engage in lobbying with state and local leaders, and provide members direct access to policymakers and decision-makers.

Yes, chambers host extensive networking opportunities throughout the year. Business After Hours are monthly evening events at member locations featuring networking, raffle prizes, small bites, and drinks. Coffee Connections provide morning networking over beverages in intimate settings. Greater Nashua offers "Out of the Box" monthly lunches for easy networking transitions. Greater Concord hosts Chamber Connections biannual meet-up groups and Leadership Greater Concord programs. BIA hosts the Annual Dinner and Awards Celebration - New Hampshire's largest business networking event. Many chambers also organize ribbon cuttings, grand openings, Business Resource Group meetings, industry-specific gatherings, and young professionals networks.

Chambers provide comprehensive economic development support for business expansion and relocation. Hampton Area Chamber was instrumental in establishing the Coastal Economic Development Corporation (CEDC) serving 10 Seacoast communities with financing and technical assistance. Chamber Collaborative of Greater Portsmouth connects businesses with Granite State Development Corporation for SBA real estate loans and Seacoast SCORE for mentoring. Greater Concord offers business resources webpage with funding opportunities and free courses. Chambers provide site selection assistance, workforce resource connections, navigation of incentive programs, access to higher education partnerships for skilled employees, and introductions to local/state officials, developers, and utilities.

New Hampshire chambers represent diverse industries across the economy. BIA members include advanced manufacturing, high technology, professional services, financial services, health care, hospitality and tourism, public utilities, higher education, and insurance. The Upper Valley region features medical and high-tech firms (anchored by Dartmouth College and Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center), precision manufacturers, food industry, arts scene, and recreational ventures. Seacoast chambers serve hospitality, tourism, and coastal recreation businesses. North Country chambers support outdoor recreation, tourism, and natural resource industries. Each chamber serves businesses of all sizes from home-based operations to large corporations.

Chambers prioritize workforce development through multiple initiatives. BIA's 2024 priorities include supporting higher quality educational and training opportunities, increasing Career and Technical Education (CTE), and promoting experiential learning, apprenticeships, and internships. Chambers partner with higher education institutions including UNH, Dartmouth College, and community colleges to develop skilled employee pipelines. They host job fairs connecting employers with talent, advocate for training program funding, and support efforts to attract and retain new talent to New Hampshire. Greater Concord's Leadership Greater Concord program has introduced 650+ community members to civic activity over 30 years. Workforce development consistently ranks as a top priority in chamber public policy roundtables.

Startups access extensive resources through NH SBDC's no-cost business advising on financing, operations, and launching businesses. SBDC's LeAF program provides up to 5 hours free consulting with Legal, Accounting, or Financial professionals. SBDC's Pathway to Micro program offers specialized assistance through Micro Business Collaborative. Seacoast SCORE provides free mentoring from 50+ experienced business professionals. Chambers offer home-based business membership options (Southern NH Chamber), Business Resource Expos connecting with SBA and other organizations, and Young Professionals networks. Greater Concord provides online business resources hub with childcare services, funding sources, and free courses. Regional chambers host Small Business Connections events and connect entrepreneurs with capital resources and training programs.
