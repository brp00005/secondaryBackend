State Chamber of Commerce

Address: 428 E. Capitol Ave., Jefferson City, MO 65101

Phone: (573) 634-3511

As Missouri's largest and most influential business association, the Missouri Chamber is the leading voice in the state capitol. Together with the Missouri Chamber Federation, they represent more than 75,000 employers across the state.

Key Services & Programs:

  • Legislative Advocacy: Aggressive lobbying team testifying on more issues than any other business organization in Missouri

  • Missouri Chamber Federation: Partnership with local chambers unifying advocacy efforts in Jefferson City and Washington, D.C.
  • Legal Reform: Protection against excessive litigation and workers' compensation insurance advocacy
  • Education & Workforce Development: Programs supporting skilled workforce development
  • International Trade: Export assistance and foreign market identification
  • Women's Council: Resource center supporting Missouri women in pursuing economic goals

Membership Benefits:

  • Voice representing 75,000+ Missouri employers in policy decisions
  • Access to Chamber Benefit Plan health insurance for 2-50 eligible employees
  • Discounted rates for events, seminars, and facility rentals
  • Product discounts and market analysis services
  • Assistance with regulatory issues
  • Network of industry and governmental leaders
  • Participation in Missouri 2030 strategic initiatives

Frequently Asked Questions

The Missouri Chamber of Commerce and Industry is the state's largest and most influential business association, founded in 1921. It represents more than 75,000 employers across Missouri through its membership and Missouri Chamber Federation partnership. The Chamber provides aggressive legislative advocacy in Jefferson City, lobbies on more issues than any other business organization in the state, and offers valuable member benefits including the Chamber Benefit Plan for health insurance. Their mission is to protect and advance Missouri employers through effective representation before the Missouri General Assembly, government agencies, and courts to reduce taxes, limit regulation, improve education, and foster economic development opportunities.

According to the U.S. Small Business Administration, Missouri has 530,380 small businesses, which represent 99.4% of all businesses in the state. These small businesses employ approximately 1.2 million Missourians, making up 46.2% of the state's workforce. Small businesses are the backbone of Missouri's economy, spanning diverse industries from agriculture and manufacturing to technology and professional services. The Missouri Chamber and local chambers across the state provide essential support services, advocacy, and networking opportunities to help these businesses succeed and grow.

The Missouri Chamber Benefit Plan is a health care coverage program designed specifically for small businesses with 2 to 50 eligible employees. It uses a MEWA (Multiple Employer Welfare Arrangement) model allowing small business employers to join together in a self-funded pool to share overall claims risk, making health insurance more affordable. The plan is administered by Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield and powered by the Missouri Chamber Federation. Members can access medical, dental, and vision benefits, with potential discounts for bundling coverage. The Eastern Region offers fully integrated Health Savings Accounts through Central Bank. A network of certified insurance brokers trained by the Chamber provides detailed quotes and helps businesses save money while giving employees access to quality health care.

The Missouri Chamber Federation is a partnership between the Missouri Chamber of Commerce and Industry and local chambers across the state designed to unite advocacy efforts in Jefferson City and Washington, D.C. The Federation creates a network of Missouri's strongest chambers of commerce, providing grassroots support and unified voice for business interests. Together with the state Chamber, the Federation represents more than 75,000 employers statewide. Federation members benefit from coordinated advocacy initiatives, shared resources, professional development opportunities through the Chamber of Commerce Executives of Missouri (CCEM), and collective bargaining power for programs like the Chamber Benefit Plan. The Federation strengthens the connection between local business needs and state-level policy decisions.

Missouri 2030 is a bold, comprehensive 15-year strategic plan developed by the Missouri Chamber of Commerce and Industry to help establish Missouri as a leader in several key economic measurements. The initiative brings together business leaders, government officials, and community stakeholders to set long-term economic vision and goals for the state. Missouri 2030 focuses on critical areas including workforce development, infrastructure improvement, education quality, tax competitiveness, regulatory environment, and innovation capacity. Chamber members can actively participate in shaping Missouri's economic future by getting involved in Missouri 2030 working groups and initiatives. The plan emphasizes measurable outcomes and accountability to ensure Missouri remains competitive in attracting and retaining businesses while improving quality of life for residents.

The Missouri Major Metro Coalition is a partnership between the four largest chambers in Missouri: the St. Louis Regional Chamber of Commerce, the Greater Kansas City Chamber of Commerce, the Springfield Area Chamber of Commerce, and the Missouri Chamber of Commerce and Industry. This coalition provides the business community a more powerful and unified voice in Jefferson City on state legislative and regulatory matters. By combining the resources and influence of Missouri's major metropolitan business organizations, the Coalition ensures that business priorities from across the state receive strong representation in policy decisions. The Coalition focuses on issues affecting large-scale economic development, workforce policies, infrastructure investment, and business climate improvements that benefit all regions of Missouri.

Rural Missouri businesses have access to extensive support through multiple programs and organizations. The Show-Me Network brings together entrepreneurs and community leaders from small and rural communities across Missouri, with at least 18 chambers of commerce and economic development groups serving as community partners. Goldman Sachs' $100 million Investment in Rural Communities provides $75 million to community development financial institutions (CDFIs) for small business loans, $15 million for business education programs through State Fair Community College, and $10 million in capacity-building grants. The Missouri Rural Enterprise & Innovation Center, Ozarks Small Business Incubator, and Codefi provide startup support and innovation ecosystem development. MU Extension's Connecting Entrepreneurial Communities program offers twice-monthly webinars on rural economic development topics. USDA Rural Development provides loans and grants for business, housing, and community programs. Local chambers throughout rural Missouri offer networking, advocacy, and business development resources tailored to small-town needs.

You can find your local Missouri chamber of commerce through several resources. The U.S. Chamber of Commerce offers an interactive Chamber Finder tool at uschamber.com/co/chambers/missouri where you can search by city or region. The Missouri Chamber of Commerce website (mochamber.com) provides links to Federation member chambers across the state. County government websites often list local chambers serving their communities - for example, Jackson County and Jefferson County maintain chamber directories on their official sites. Regional planning commissions like the Meramec Regional Planning Commission list chambers in their service areas. You can also search online for "[Your City] Missouri Chamber of Commerce" to find contact information. Most chambers maintain websites with membership information, event calendars, and business directories. If you're unsure which chamber serves your area, contact the Missouri Chamber of Commerce at (573) 634-3511 for referral assistance.

Missouri offers comprehensive economic development resources through multiple state and regional organizations. The Missouri Department of Economic Development (ded.mo.gov) provides programs supporting business growth, workforce development, and community development. Missouri Partnership (missouripartnership.com) offers complimentary support for businesses expanding into Missouri, including site selection assistance, incentive program navigation, and workforce solutions. The Missouri Works Program provides financial incentives for job creation and capital investment. Missouri One Start offers customized recruitment strategies and no-cost training for eligible businesses. Missouri Small Business Development Centers (sbdc.missouri.edu) deliver free counseling and technical assistance across the state. Missouri Enterprise assists manufacturers with technology and process improvements. The Missouri Technology Corporation provides strategic investments to high-tech companies. Regional organizations like the Economic Development Corporation of Kansas City and local chambers offer targeted support for businesses in their areas. Export assistance is available through DED's international trade programs. The Women's Council connects women entrepreneurs to advisory services and resources.

The Missouri Chamber of Commerce and Industry provides the most aggressive and professional lobbying team among Missouri business organizations, testifying on more issues more often than any other group in the state capitol. During the 2025 legislative session alone, the Chamber's advocacy work resulted in an estimated $1.1 billion impact for Missouri businesses. The Chamber represents employers before the Missouri General Assembly, government agencies, and courts on critical issues including tax reduction, regulatory limitation, education improvement, legal reform, workers' compensation insurance, health care policy, workforce development, international trade, state transportation funding, and employment law. No other pro-business organization matches the Chamber's reach and influence in Jefferson City. Through the Missouri Chamber Federation, local chambers across the state unite their grassroots advocacy efforts with state-level lobbying. The Missouri Major Metro Coalition amplifies the voice of business interests in policy decisions. The Chamber also maintains a Political Action Committee (PAC) supporting pro-business candidates. Members gain direct access to this powerful advocacy network representing 75,000+ employers.
