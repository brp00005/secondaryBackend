West Virginia Chamber of Commerce

Statewide Business Advocacy

The West Virginia Chamber of Commerce is the largest and most influential general business organization in the state, representing all business sectors across every region. For nearly nine decades, the Chamber has been at the forefront of addressing critical issues including education, economic development, and workforce readiness.

Members range from small business enterprises to mid-size manufacturers, tourism destinations, energy companies, and Fortune 500 corporations. Small businesses form the core of the membership, making up 95 percent of member companies and firms.

Business Advocacy: The Chamber ensures powerful representation in state politics, actively championing member interests in regulatory affairs, legislative decisions, and court proceedings. Members collectively employ over half of West Virginia's workforce.

Key Programs and Initiatives

  • Business Climate Reports: Annual reports outlining the impact of state policies on the economy
  • Workforce Development: Programs to attract, retain, and grow jobs for West Virginia's workforce
  • Drug-Free Workplace Program: Resources for creating drug-responsible workplaces
  • Student Programs: Enhancing graduation and post-secondary prospects through business partnerships
  • Business Academy: Educational webinars and certification tools through legal and industry experts
  • Local Chamber Network: Coordinating more than 40 local chambers across the state

Several economic development organizations work closely with West Virginia chambers of commerce to support business growth and economic prosperity:

West Virginia Economic Development Authority (WVEDA): Promotes state business prosperity and economic welfare by offering loans and financial assistance to manufacturing businesses and industrial process entities. The Authority can borrow money, issue bonds, and provide credit enhancements.

West Virginia Economic Development Council (WVEDC): Promotes activities to retain and expand current business and industry in West Virginia and to attract new business and industry to the state. WVEDC is the only professional organization in the state with the sole function of promoting economic development and serving economic development professionals.

WVU Bureau of Business & Economic Research: Since 1948, serving the people of West Virginia by providing the state's business, policymaking, and advocacy communities with reliable and timely data as well as rigorous applied economic analysis to help design and implement better business practices and public policies.

Regional Development Authorities: Organizations like the New River Gorge Regional Development Authority and Eastern WV Economic Alliance that coordinate extensive economic development partner networks including chambers, business leaders, workforce training organizations, schools, and government agencies.

West Virginia Division of Economic Development: State agency providing business development resources, site selection assistance, and incentive programs.

Businesses in West Virginia with fewer than 50 full-time employees are not required to offer group health insurance coverage. If your business has 50 or more full-time employees, it is considered an applicable large employer, which means that you may face penalties if you do not offer health insurance benefits under the Affordable Care Act.

West Virginia law does not have any additional requirements beyond the federal ACA mandate.

Small Business Health Insurance Options:

  • To qualify for group health insurance in West Virginia, your company typically needs at least two employees including the owner
  • Employers with between 1 and 50 full-time equivalent employees can offer insurance through SHOP (Small Business Health Options Program)
  • On average, small employers in West Virginia cover 77% for single employees and 62% for families, costing approximately $448 monthly for individuals and $1,072 for families

Tax Benefits: Small businesses with fewer than 10 full-time employees that pay average annual wages of $25,000 or less may qualify for the full tax credit. The amount of the credit is reduced for companies with more workers and higher wages, phasing out entirely for those with 25 or more workers.

Alternative Options: Small companies can assist and manage expenses by providing Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) or healthcare Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs). 36% of West Virginia's small businesses have an HSA, while 44% have an FSA.
