Frequently Asked Questions

The Colorado Chamber of Commerce (cochamber.com) focuses on statewide business advocacy and policy issues at the state legislature, while the Denver Metro Chamber of Commerce (denverchamber.org) serves the metro Denver region specifically with local advocacy, economic development, and networking. The Denver Metro Chamber, celebrating 157 years of service, is one of the largest regional chambers. Many businesses join both organizations to benefit from both state-level advocacy and local business development programs.

Colorado has over 100 local chambers of commerce serving communities across its 64 counties. The Colorado Chamber Alliance connects local chambers throughout the state. This includes major metropolitan chambers like Denver Metro Chamber, South Metro Denver Chamber, Colorado Springs Chamber & EDC, Boulder Chamber, and Fort Collins Area Chamber, as well as mountain resort chambers in communities like Vail, Aspen, Breckenridge, and Steamboat Springs. There are also specialty chambers serving diverse business communities.

Chamber membership benefits include networking opportunities with business leaders and potential customers, advocacy representation in government affairs, marketing exposure through directories and events, professional development through workshops and leadership programs, member discounts on business services, enhanced business credibility, referral opportunities, and access to economic development resources. Colorado chambers also often provide connections to tourism promotion, workforce development programs, and industry-specific resources.

Chamber membership costs vary significantly depending on the chamber size, business size, and membership level selected. Small local chambers may charge $200-500 annually for basic membership, while larger metropolitan chambers like Denver Metro Chamber may range from $500-3,000+ depending on company size and benefits selected. Mountain resort area chambers often have seasonal tourism business rates. Most chambers offer tiered membership levels with increasing benefits. Contact your local chamber directly for current pricing.

The Colorado Chamber Alliance is a network of local chambers of commerce throughout Colorado that partner with the Colorado Chamber of Commerce on statewide advocacy and business initiatives. Member chambers collaborate on legislative priorities, share best practices, and provide a unified voice for business interests across the state. The Alliance helps local chambers connect their members to state-level resources and policy discussions while maintaining their local community focus.

Yes, Colorado's ski resort and mountain communities have active chambers and tourism organizations including: Vail Valley Partnership, Aspen Chamber Resort Association, Breckenridge Tourism Office, Steamboat Springs Chamber, Telluride Tourism Board, Winter Park & Fraser Chamber, Crested Butte Chamber, and Durango Area Tourism Office. These organizations often combine traditional chamber services with destination marketing, tourism promotion, and seasonal business support specific to resort community needs.

To find your local chamber in Colorado: 1) Visit cochamber.com/about-us/local-chambers for the Colorado Chamber Alliance directory, 2) Search the U.S. Chamber of Commerce directory at uschamber.com/co/chambers/colorado, 3) Search online for "[your city name] chamber of commerce Colorado," 4) Contact your city or county economic development office, or 5) Check with regional EDCs like Metro Denver EDC. Most Colorado communities have at least one chamber or business association.

Colorado has several diversity chambers including: Colorado Black Chamber of Commerce (headquartered in Denver), Colorado Hispanic Chamber of Commerce, Asian Chamber of Commerce Colorado, Colorado LGBT Chamber of Commerce, and Colorado Women's Chamber. These organizations provide specialized networking, certification assistance for supplier diversity programs, advocacy, and resources tailored to diverse business owners. Many host annual events and connect members to corporate supplier diversity opportunities.

The Denver Metro Chamber of Commerce, led by President/CEO J.J. Ament, offers extensive resources including: advocacy at local, state, and federal levels; economic development through the Metro Denver EDC partnership; leadership programs through the Chamber Leadership Foundation; networking events and business mixers; workforce development initiatives; member directory and marketing opportunities; and policy research on issues affecting Colorado businesses. The Chamber has $12.7 million in annual revenue and has been serving the region for 157 years.

Colorado chambers support small businesses through: business development workshops and seminars, networking events connecting small businesses with larger companies, advocacy for small business-friendly policies, connections to Colorado SBDC (Small Business Development Center) resources, assistance with permits and licensing information, access to group benefits programs, marketing and promotional opportunities, mentorship programs, ribbon-cutting ceremonies for new businesses, and referrals. Many chambers also partner with OEDIT (Office of Economic Development) to help businesses access state programs and incentives.
